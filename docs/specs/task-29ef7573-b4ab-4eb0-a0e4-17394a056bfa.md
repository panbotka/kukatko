# Show the User's Content, Not the System's Labels

Two places where Kukátko shows a machine's name for something instead of showing the thing itself. Both undermine an app whose entire job is looking at family photos.

## Part 1 — The people page has no faces on it

`/people` (`web/src/pages/PeoplePage.tsx`) renders a tile per subject. In the live catalogue 8 of 9 subjects show a grey placeholder reading "Bez náhledu" — a page about people with no people on it. The single subject that does have a cover shows the FULL photo (a wide shot of someone sitting in a chair), so you still cannot tell who it is.

The data to fix this already exists. The `markers` table holds a face box per photo per subject — normalised `x`, `y`, `w`, `h`, plus `score` and `invalid` (migration `0008_subjects_markers.sql`), and the live catalogue has 47 markers. `web/src/lib/faceGeometry.ts` (`padBbox`, `faceBoxStyle`) and `components/people/FaceOverlay.tsx` already position boxes as PERCENTAGES over a photo — the same maths crops to a face instead of outlining one, so no new backend thumbnail work is needed.

### Requirements

- Every subject tile shows a CROP OF THAT PERSON'S FACE, derived automatically from their markers, done in the browser from an existing photo thumbnail using the marker's normalised box.
- Choose the marker deliberately and document the rule in a comment: prefer a large, high-`score`, non-`invalid` face. The next reader must be able to tell why a given face won.
- An explicitly set `cover_photo_uid` on the subject still WINS. The automatic crop is a fallback for subjects with no cover, never an override.
- Pad the crop so it reads as a portrait rather than a tight box on the nose — `padBbox` exists for exactly this.
- A subject with no usable marker keeps the existing placeholder. Do not invent a face.
- Never distort: a non-square marker box shown in a square tile needs proper cover-cropping, not stretching.
- Same treatment on the photo detail page: the "Lidé" list in the "Uspořádání" card shows bare text ("Nepojmenovaný obličej 1", "Nepojmenovaný obličej 2"). Give each face chip its face thumbnail so the reader can tell WHO without opening anything.

## Part 2 — The photo detail is titled with a filename

`web/src/pages/PhotoDetailPage.tsx` renders the FILENAME as the page `<h1>` — e.g. "IMG_8423.jpeg". That is the camera's name for the photo, not the user's.

Below the photo, the metadata card shows four fields — Název, Popis, Poznámka AI, Poznámky — and ALL FOUR render the identical italic grey placeholder "Přidat…". It is a column of identical placeholders that says nothing about what each field is for or why anyone would fill it in. The edit pencils sit at the far right margin, a long way from the label they belong to.

### Requirements

- The `<h1>` shows a HUMAN identity: the photo's title when it has one; otherwise fall back to something the photo actually has — its capture date, plus its place when known. A filename is not an identity.
- The filename does not disappear — it moves into `components/photo/TechnicalDetails.tsx`, where a filename belongs.
- Replace the four identical "Přidat…" placeholders with per-field prompts that say what that field is for. A label labels; a placeholder invites or demonstrates — it should not repeat the label's job. Each field gets its own text.
- "Poznámka AI" is jargon. The app's stated audience is non-technical people who should "never feel intimidated" — rename it to something a non-developer reads without pausing.
- The capture date reads "10. 7. 2026 23:03:40". Nobody needs seconds — show minute precision; the exact value stays in technical details.
- Move the edit affordance next to the field it edits instead of parking it at the right margin.

## Constraints

- All user-facing text via i18n, Czech + English parity — `i18n.test.ts` enforces it. No hardcoded strings.
- Icons only from `bootstrap-icons` via the `Icon` component, decorative ones `aria-hidden`, always paired with a visible label.
- Use the existing token layer `web/src/styles/tokens.css`: `.kk-page-title` for the h1, `.kk-tile`, `--kk-radius-*`, `--kk-space-*`. Do NOT put `h3`/`h5`/`fs-*` utilities or inline `fontSize` on headings — the token layer owns type. Do not redefine Bootstrap `--bs-*` variables.
- Keep focus rings and the 44px touch floor under `@media (pointer: coarse)`.
- `docs/UX_AUDIT.md` is STALE — it predates `tokens.css` and its `h1.h3` advice is superseded. Where it disagrees with `tokens.css`, tokens win.
